DBT Concepts & Training Exercises

Introduction to DBT
Concept/Skill Resource Description
Emotional Flooding 11-minute YouTube video This one isn't directly about DBT, but it sets the stage for us. A therapist uses the Hulk to explain emotional flooding and how unchecked anger hijacks our body and mind, sabotaging our goals and relationships. On the flip side, it highlights anger's value as a signal, alerting us to deeper issues and emotions that deserve our attention.
What is DBT? 4-minute YouTube video A brief overview of the four skill sets you learn in DBT: mindfulness, distress tolerance, emotional regulation, interpersonal effectiveness.
Wise Mind Handout Describes emotional mind and reasonable mind; illustrates how wise mind is the integration of both. This concept is central to DBT.
Mindfulness - The Foundational Skill 3-minute YouTube video Psychologist who came up with DBT talks about the influence from Zen Buddhism.
Wise Mind 3-minute YouTube video This animated video does a great job explaining the different roles of emotion mind and reasonable mind, and how the goal of mindfulness is to help you re-engage with your wise mind to make decisions that are aligned with your goals and values.
Mindfulness Training Exercises
Skill Resource Description
What & How Skills Handout Mindfulness is all about paying attention to the present moment, without judging or trying to change it. This handout goes over the 'what' skills of mindfulness (as in what you're doing - observing, describing, participating), as well as the 'how' skills (as in how your doing it - nonjudgmentally, one-mindfully, effectively).
Single Object Mindfulness 7-min YouTube video Suggestion: Watch this video once through and just try focusing on the object shown on-screen. Then, repeat the exercise using a real-life object, but listen along to the audio to guide you.

This is a training exercise that will help you build the muscle of one-mindedness, but you can also use it in the moment, when you are feeling emotionally flooded.
Focusing Attention through Body Scanning 5-min YouTube video Just as the guide says in the intro, the purpose of this exercise isn't to relax, but rather to focus your attention on and bring awareness to what's happening in your body. This skill is essential to noticing the physical cues of emotional flooding when they start to emerge.
Noticing without Judgment 3-min YouTube video This exercise is designed to help you build the skill of mindfulness without judgment. The idea is to resist the urge to label your experience as 'good' or 'bad', and not try to change it or push it away. This is considered on one of the 'how' skills of mindfulness. Check out the DBT 'What' & 'How' Skills handout for more on this.
